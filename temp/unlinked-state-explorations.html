If you want one to ship.

Combining structural + glyphic gives you a state language that survives grayscale, small sizes, focus rings, and dense panels. The strongest pair from these explorations is:

  • 01 — Open-bracket edge as the unlinked treatment, because the broken-seam metaphor matches the underlying mental model (the link was here, it isn't anymore) and the silhouette change reads even at 50% opacity. Pair with the existing amber.
  • 03 — Inline broken-chain glyph if you also want a positive signal for linked rows (a solid chain glyph in the same slot). This makes the linked/unlinked distinction symmetrical instead of "absence of linked = unlinked".
  • Reserve 07 — corner badge for cases where the broken link is actionable urgency (e.g. the referenced token was deleted), so the badge isn't blown by routine "user opted out of the link" state.

Re: your specific question about breaking the outline on the left only — variant 01 is exactly that, and adding the two short ticks at the top/bottom of the gap (instead of a clean removal) is what stops it from looking like a rendering bug. Variant 02 is the same idea executed as a dashed line — softer, less expressive.
